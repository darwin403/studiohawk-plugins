# Completeness & Compliance Checklist

The fact-check track (`audit-checklist.md`) asks *"are the claims true against the data?"*. This
track asks a different question: *"is this a complete, on-brand, compliant release that's ready to
go out?"* — and it runs on **every** press release, whatever its type (data-led, expert-led, UGC).

These are mostly **presence and alignment** checks, not recalculations. Report each as **present**,
**missing**, or **flag** with the specific issue and fix. Where an item can't be confirmed from what
you were given (e.g. formatting isn't visible, an asset lives outside the doc), say so rather than
passing it silently — an unconfirmed item is not a clean item.

## A. Accompanying assets

1. **Pitch attached.** Every press release must ship with an accompanying pitch (the outreach note
   to journalists). Confirm one is attached to the thread/doc. Flag if there's no pitch.
2. **Imagery included.** There must be at least one image with the release. Flag if none is present.
3. **Link to imagery in the PR.** Beyond the image itself, the PR should contain a working link to
   the imagery (the Drive/Dropbox/gallery link a journalist pulls assets from). Flag if the link is
   missing or points nowhere.

## B. Client voice & expert attribution

4. **Client quote present.** Data-led, expert-led, and UGC releases should **all** carry a client
   quote — it's the client's voice and the journalist's link back to them. Flag if there's no quote
   attributed to the client.
5. **Every expert fully credited.** For each expert cited, confirm all three: a **name**, a
   **title**, and a **linked headshot**. Flag any expert missing any of the three, naming which is
   absent.

## C. Brand alignment

6. **Ties back to the client's offering.** The story's angle must clearly connect to what the brand
   actually sells — and must not undercut it. (Example: a Tiny Away piece about recreating the
   "cabin experience at home" undercuts the offering, which is *short stays in tiny homes*.) Flag any
   angle that has no clear tie-in to the client's offering, or that works against it, and say why.
7. **Client introduced properly on first mention.** The first reference to the client should be a
   descriptive introduction, not the bare name — e.g. "Australian eco-tourism brand Tiny Away",
   not just "Tiny Away". Flag a bare first mention and suggest a descriptor drawn from what the
   brand actually is.
8. **Client logo in the header.** The client's logo must be present in the header of the release.
   Flag if it's missing.

## D. Formatting

9. **Archivo, size 11, throughout.** Body copy should be in the Archivo typeface at size 11, applied
   consistently across the whole document. Flag any section in a different font or size. If the
   document format you're reading doesn't expose font/size, say the check couldn't be confirmed and
   ask the reviewer to verify in the doc rather than marking it clean.

## E. Legal & compliance

10. **Legally sound and compliant.** Scan for obvious legal risk and flag it for the reviewer (you
    are not giving legal advice — you're surfacing risk and recommending review):
    - Unsubstantiated or absolute claims that could read as misleading/false advertising
      ("guaranteed", "#1", "cures", "safest") without backing.
    - Statements that could defame or disparage a named competitor.
    - Imagery, logos, or quotes used without evident rights/permission.
    - Personal or private information used without consent.
    - Any required disclaimer, T&Cs, or attribution that's missing.
    Recommend a legal/compliance check on anything uncertain rather than declaring it sound.

## Reporting these

Fold the results into the chat report under a **Completeness & compliance** section (see
`audit-checklist.md` §4): list each flagged item with the specific problem and the fix, and give an
explicit all-clear for the checks that passed. Presence checks that you genuinely couldn't confirm
are listed as *unconfirmed*, not clean.
